Why not wait

Estonian law (TTOS § 13 lg 1) requires an occupational health check within four months of an employee starting work. For a foreign worker, this means family-doctor registration AND occupational health check are both first-six-months issues.

Doing them together through one provider:

  • reduces administrative burden
  • lowers the risk of one step being missed on time
  • gives you all required documents from one place if the Labour Inspectorate audits you
Heads-up: Labour Inspectorate fines for occupational health non-compliance can reach €32,000 for legal entities.

FAQ

Does €99 apply per employee?

Yes, the price is per employee. 10 new foreign workers = €990 total. For larger volumes (20+ at once), ask for a custom quote.

Does the employee need to come in person?

The occupational health check happens at Valvekliinik in Ülemiste. Family-doctor registration is arranged by us — the employee needs to attend the first family-doctor appointment, which we schedule at a convenient time.

What languages are appointments conducted in?

Estonian, English, Russian. A Finnish-speaking doctor is also available. We ask about preferred language at booking.

What if our employee is an EU citizen and already has Estonian health insurance?

Same process. The offer applies to anyone born abroad needing family-doctor registration in Estonia.

How is this different from middlemen charging €100 for "the same" service?

The middleman finds you a family doctor — that's it. Through us you get family-doctor registration plus occupational health check and documentation for the same price. One provider, one contract.

Can you also arrange our company's full occupational health contract?

Yes. After we handle your foreign worker's paperwork, we can quote a complete occupational health service for your wider team.
