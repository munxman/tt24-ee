Welcome — your family doctor in Estonia is ready

Your employer has registered you with our partner SL Tervisekeskus family medicine clinic. This page explains what that means, how to use it, and what to do next.

What this means for you

In Estonia, every legal resident has the right to a family doctor (perearst). The family doctor is your first point of contact for all general health issues — illness, prescriptions, referrals to specialists, vaccinations, mental health.

Because most Estonian family doctors are not accepting new patients, your employer arranged your registration with SL Tervisekeskus in Tallinn, which is part of the Valvekliinik medical network. You are now officially their patient.

What to do now

1

Save the contact info

Add the phone number above to your phone now. You'll need it next time you're sick or need a prescription.

2

Wait for the occupational health appointment

Estonian law requires every new employee to have an occupational health check within 4 months of starting work. We will contact your employer to schedule it — you'll receive a date, time and location.

3

Book an appointment when you need it

For general health issues (illness, prescription refills, check-ups), call +372 5 911 0909 and ask for an appointment with your family doctor.

4

Use the emergency line if it's urgent

For urgent but non-life-threatening issues outside office hours, call 1220 — Estonia's family doctor advice line, available 24/7 in Estonian, Russian and English. For life-threatening emergencies, call 112.

Languages

Our doctors and staff can serve you in Estonian, English, Russian. Finnish is also available. If you need another language, tell us when booking and we'll arrange interpretation if possible.

Health insurance (haigekassa)

If you have a legal work contract in Estonia and your employer pays social tax, you are entitled to Estonian health insurance through Tervisekassa (formerly Haigekassa). This covers most family doctor visits, prescriptions, hospital stays and specialist referrals.

Your insurance typically becomes active 14 days after your employer registers you. You can check your status at www.eesti.ee using your ID-card or Mobile-ID.

Common questions

Do I need to come to the clinic in person?

For the first visit and the occupational health check, yes. After that, many issues can be handled by phone or video — including prescription refills and quick consultations.

How much does it cost?

If you have Estonian health insurance (haigekassa), family doctor visits are free. There may be a small co-payment for some services. Specialist visits referred by your family doctor are also covered.

What if I move to another city?

You can register with a family doctor in your new city. Tell us if you're moving so we can transfer your records properly.

What if I leave Estonia?

Your registration ends when you stop being a legal resident. No paperwork is required from you.

Can I bring my family?

Yes, if they are also legal residents in Estonia. Contact us to register family members separately.
